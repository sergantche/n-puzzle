# Literature survey: sliding puzzle (N×M)

Short survey of solvability criteria and proofs. Focus: rectangular board, one blank, legal move = swap blank with an edge neighbor.

---

## 1. Classics (1879)

**Johnson, W. W.; Story, W. E.** *Notes on the "15" Puzzle.* American Journal of Mathematics **2**(4), 397–404, 1879.  
DOI: [10.2307/2369492](https://doi.org/10.2307/2369492) · PDF: [Rutgers scan](https://sites.math.rutgers.edu/~zeilberg/Hist21/15puzzle.pdf)

Two parts of one paper:

| Author | Result |
|--------|--------|
| **Johnson** | *Necessity:* cannot move between configurations of opposite “order” (parity). Explains why Loyd’s 14–15 challenge is impossible. |
| **Story** | *Sufficiency:* every admissible even configuration reduces to the goal; construction via cycling three tiles along a closed tour on the board. |

**Invariant (equivalent forms):**

- parity of the permutation of all cells (including blank) + parity of taxicab distance of blank to goal;
- for list $L$ (row-major, blank omitted) and blank row $r_B$ from the bottom: if $M$ is even, $I(L) + r_B \equiv 1 \pmod 2$; if $M$ is odd, $I(L) \equiv 0 \pmod 2$ (README formula).

Historical note: the puzzle fad peaked in the US in 1879; the math preceded Loyd’s prize stunt.

## 3. General $m \times n$

| Source | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Johnson–Story (1879), Story’s continuation | $r\times c$ boards; “first/second kind”; reversed tile order on $4\times 4$. |
| Wikipedia / HandWiki | For $m,n \ge 2$, all *even* permutations are solvable; induction from $2\times 2$. |
| **Archer (1999)**, *A modern treatment of the 15 puzzle* | Elementary proof via Hamiltonian path on the cell graph. |
| **Calabro (UCSD)**, [*Solving the 15-Puzzle*](https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~ccalabro/essays/15_puzzle.pdf) | Explicit $m\times n$ criterion $\mathrm{sign}(\sigma) = \mathrm{sign}(d(p,B))$; algorithm; lower bound $\Omega((m+n)mn)$ moves. |

**README link:** Calabro Lemmas 1–2 = necessity + sufficiency via sign and blank distance; with $B$ = bottom-right corner, equivalent to $I(L)$ and $r_B$.

## 4. Groups and generators (sufficiency key)

Standard chain for 15-puzzle with blank at bottom-right:

1. Reachable configs with fixed blank $\Rightarrow$ even permutations $A_{15}$.
2. $A_n$ generated by 3-cycles (Mathlib: `closure_three_cycles_eq_alternating`).
3. One explicit 3-cycle from corner macro (`cornerRotCfg` / commutator $URU^{-1}R^{-1}$ in [Mena–Sinclair](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.07106)).
4. Conjugation yields all 3-cycles $\Rightarrow$ all of $A_{15}$.

**Mena & Sinclair (2019).** *Solution of the 15 puzzle problem.* [arXiv:1908.07106](https://arxiv.org/abs/1908.07106) — random walks on $A_{n^2-1}$; move commutator = 3-cycle.
